Thermodynamics
                                                                𝜕
w = p*dV --(1)
q-w = dU
q = dU + pdV --(2)
H = U + PV --(3)
Cv = 𝜕U/𝜕T |V
Cp = 𝜕H/𝜕T |P
for ideal gases 𝜕U/𝜕V = 0

First law:

u=f(t,v)
dU = (𝜕U/𝜕T)*dT|V + (𝜕U/𝜕V)*dV|T        // for ideal gases 𝜕U/𝜕V = 0
--> q = (𝜕U/𝜕T)*dT|V + {p+(𝜕U/𝜕V)|T}*dV     // Cv = 𝜕U/𝜕T |V

--> first law for ideal gas : q= n*Cv*dT + P*dV
or : q = (𝜕U/𝜕T)*dT|V + {p+(𝜕U/𝜕V)|T}*dV

H=U+PV  //enthalpy(H) is sum of internal energy and work done to establish volume at certain pressure
dH = q + VdP (differentiating and substituting (2))         // also, enthalpy at constant pressure is heat supplied at constant pressure
hence q/𝜕T|P = 𝜕H/𝜕T | P = Cp
Cp-Cv = 𝜕(H=U+PV)/𝜕T |P - 𝜕U/𝜕T | V
𝜕U/𝜕T |P = 𝜕U/𝜕T |V + 𝜕U/𝜕V |T * 𝜕V/𝜕T |P
Cp-Cv = (𝜕V/𝜕T |P) * (P + 𝜕U/𝜕V |T) //but for ideal gas du/dv|t is 0
so Cp-Cv=R
